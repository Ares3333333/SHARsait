# Design constraints — SHAR Production

**Non‑negotiable.** All design and front-end decisions must respect these.

---

## Do not

- **Invent a new brand** — SHAR Production is the existing brand. Do not introduce a different visual or verbal identity.
- **Make it flashy** — No neon, no aggressive glow, no attention-grabbing effects for their own sake.
- **Make it SaaS** — Avoid “startup” or product-dashboard feel: no generic SaaS typography, no widget-heavy layouts, no growth-hack tone.
- **Make it noisy** — Rest by default. No constant motion, no visual clutter, no competing elements.

---

## Do

- **Luxury‑minimal** — Few elements, clear hierarchy, restraint. Premium = less, not more.
- **Cinematic** — Typography and rhythm that feel film-like: controlled, spacious, deliberate.
- **Restrained** — One accent (brand red), limited motion, subtle surfaces. No decoration for its own sake.
- **Premium** — Every touchpoint should feel expensive and considered, not template-like.
- **Preserve SHAR Production’s real business identity** — High-end AI/cinema production, Russian market, authorial approach. The site should reflect that identity, not overwrite it.

---

## In practice

- **Typography:** One heading font, one body font. Clear roles (display / heading / body / label). No mixing in extra typefaces or “personality” fonts.
- **Color:** One brand accent (#9c0404). Variation via opacity and context, not new accent colors.
- **Motion:** Rest by default. Motion on intent (hover, focus, scroll, one-off load). No infinite decorative animations.
- **Surfaces:** Visible but subtle (borders, light fill). No “invisible” panels, no heavy glass or effects.
- **Copy and content:** Do not invent new positioning or tone. Refine and clarify within the existing business identity.
